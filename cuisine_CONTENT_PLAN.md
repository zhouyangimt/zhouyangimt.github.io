## C02 — The Wok (full structure)

**One-liner:** Most Western homes don't have gas. So you need a flat-bottom carbon steel wok. $40. Light. Gets more non-stick every time you use it.

Sections:
1. Opening — You bought 11 sauces. Now you need the tool to cook them.
2. Why a wok — heat, speed, wok hei
3. **Round vs Flat Bottom** — Gas → round + wok ring. Electric/induction → flat only. Most Western kitchens are electric. Flat still produces wok hei.
4. Carbon steel vs everything else — cast iron too heavy, non-stick can't handle wok temps
5. Size & handles — 14" sweet spot. Single handle + helper handle. Wood grip.
6. Seasoning — bluing + oil. Works on any stove. Induction skip bluing if needed.
7. Care — no soap. Hot water + bamboo brush. Dry + oil coat.
8. What NOT to buy — Teflon woks, electric woks, anything over $80
9. Closing — One carbon steel wok + C01's 11 sauces = all of China in your kitchen

**Affiliate:** Round-bottom wok + Flat-bottom wok + Wok ring

## C03 — Spices (full structure)

**One-liner:** Western herbs are gentlemen. Chinese spices are madmen — Sichuan pepper electrifies your tongue, dried chilies burn your throat, star anise does both at once.

4. **Five-Spice vs Thirteen-Spice** — When to use which

6. **Cumin** — Xinjiang's wild card. Lamb skewers. Why American Chinese restaurants don't use it.
7. Closing — 7 spices, 8 combinations. Chinese flavor is spice-driven, not ingredient-driven.

**Affiliate:** Sichuan peppercorn, dried chili mix, thirteen-spice, white pepper, cumin powder

## C04 — Vinegars (full structure)

**One-liner:** Italians talk about balsamic. Shanxi people have been making vinegar from sorghum for 4,000 years. C01 introduced Chinkiang. Now open the door.

**Note:** This article is educational/traffic play, not pure conversion. Amazon availability for Shanxi aged vinegar is poor.

Sections:
1. Opening — Four thousand years of vinegar, four shades of sour.
2. **Chinkiang** (recap) — Entry point. Dumpling sauce, sweet-and-sour ribs. Glutinous rice, aged like balsamic.
3. **Shanxi Aged Vinegar** — Northern king. Sorghum, 3+ years aging, smoky. Knife-cut noodles, cold skin noodles.
4. **Rice Vinegar** (white) — Everyday mild. Sweet-and-sour base, pickling, cold dishes.
5. **Red Vinegar** — Cantonese morning. Mildly sweet. Dim sum, wontons. Not essential — a window.
6. Closing — You have Chinkiang. Add Shanxi if you love northern food. Add rice vinegar if you pickle. Red can wait.

## C05 — Dried Pantry (full structure)

**One-liner:** Sauces expire. Dried goods don't. Throw three bags in your cabinet, open them six months later, hot water revives them.

Sections:
1. Opening — Preservation without refrigeration. Three thousand years of Chinese food science.
2. **Dried Shiitake** — 10x more aromatic than fresh. 20-min soak = stock. Braised chicken, claypot rice, stir-fried greens. Flower cap mushrooms (花菇).
3. **Wood Ear** — Crunchy. Flavorless. Addictive texture. Hot and sour soup, moo shu pork, cold salad. Black vs white (snow fungus for desserts).
4. **Dried Baby Shrimp** — China's secret MSG. Bloom in oil before anything else = seafood base for the whole dish. Cabbage and tofu goes from "light" to "elegant."
5. **Glass Noodles** — Ants Climbing a Tree, hot and sour noodle soup, hot pot, cold salads. Mung bean vs sweet potato starch noodles.
6. **Dried Tofu Skin (腐竹)** — Soy milk skin, dried. Rehydrates to meat-like texture. Braised, cold salad, mala dry pot.
7. **Dried Scallops (干贝)** — Luxury tier. The soul of XO sauce. One scallop soaked = seafood base for an entire pot of soup.
8. Closing — 6 dried goods + C01 sauces = all the "home cooking" flavor foundations.

**Affiliate:** Dried shiitake, wood ear, dried baby shrimp, glass noodles, dried tofu skin, dried scallops

## C06 — Cleaver (full structure)

**One-liner:** It's not an axe. It's the most versatile knife in your kitchen — slices, smashes garlic, scoops like a spatula.

Sections:
1. Opening — "Is that for chopping people?" No. It's the only knife in a Chinese home kitchen.
2. What it's NOT — Not a bone cleaver. Not a horror movie prop. It's lighter than you think — ~400g, built for slicing.
3. What it IS — Slicer + garlic smasher + bench scraper + spatula. One knife = chef's knife + paring knife + bench scraper.
4. Two types — Slicer (thin, light, daily) vs bone cleaver (thick, heavy, bones only). You only need the slicer.
5. Carbon steel vs stainless — Carbon sharper, easier to sharpen, but needs drying. Stainless convenient but less "sticky." Chinese chefs almost universally use carbon.
6. How to hold — Not a fist. Index + thumb pinch the blade, three fingers on the handle. Wrist, not arm.
7. Santoku — Sino-Japanese hybrid. If the cleaver scares you, get a Santoku. Not as versatile but closer to Chinese technique than a Western chef's knife.
8. Closing — Western chefs show off 8-knife racks. Chinese chefs pull out one — and do everything.

**Affiliate:** CCK carbon steel slicer preferred (check Amazon availability), Santoku backup, carbon steel slicer alternatives

## C07 — Steamer (full structure)

**One-liner:** Steaming is Chinese cooking's most underrated technique. A bamboo steamer — no oil, no flipping, no watching. Set, wait 10 minutes, serve.

Sections:
1. Opening — Microwave makes food hot. Bamboo steamer makes food alive.
2. Why steam — Healthy (no oil), lazy (no flipping), gentle (fish stays whole, greens stay green). Steamed fish is banquet-level.
3. Bamboo vs metal — Bamboo breathes, absorbs condensation, doesn't drip back onto food. Metal steamers drip and ruin bao texture. Bamboo isn't poverty — it's correct.
4. How to use — Set on wok with water below. Stack multiple tiers. Dumplings, buns, fish, chicken, vegetables. Food directly in steamer ≠ food on plate in steamer.
5. Three-piece system — Steamer + wok + lid = steaming station. Your C02 wok is the base. No dedicated appliance needed.
6. Care — Air dry after each use. No washing needed unless greasy. Gets better with age.
7. Closing — One wok + one steamer set = a new cooking method in your kitchen. No electricity. No counter space. Just bamboo and water vapor.

**Affiliate:** Bamboo steamer set + wok (C02 cross-link)
