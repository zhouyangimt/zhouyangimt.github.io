About

The 25th Frame

I’m zhouyang.

Thirteen years. Three companies. Microsoft. Google. NVIDIA. Free cafeterias, laundry rooms you never used, and a salary that made your parents proud. Every morning I’d walk into a glass tower, open a laptop, and build things that millions of people would use without ever knowing my name.

And every evening I’d walk out thinking: is this it?

Then I quit. No dramatic exit — just a clean break. If you’ve seen The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, you’ll remember the 25th negative. The frame photographer Sean O’Connell called “the quintessence of life.” The one worth chasing.

This blog is my 25th frame.

Why I write

I grew up in Shanghai. Shanghainese food, Hangzhou tea, the sharp bite of baijiu at family gatherings — these aren’t hobbies. They’re the soil I grew out of.

After a decade in Silicon Valley and Seattle, I noticed something that bothered me. The English-language web is full of articles about China, but almost none of them feel true. They’re written by outsiders looking in. They tell you China is a market. A competitor. A geopolitical story.

They never tell you what a bowl of Lanzhou beef noodles tastes like at 7am in Gansu, steam rising into air so cold your fingers go numb. They don’t tell you why a farmer-turned-distiller in Guizhou spends forty years perfecting a single baijiu recipe he’ll never sell outside his village. They don’t tell you why the first sip of Longjing every spring makes a Hangzhou local stop talking mid-sentence and close their eyes.

I want to fix that.

For my Chinese friends: I’m sharing everything I learned inside those glass towers — AI, programming, indie hacking, building things from scratch. In Chinese. No jargon. Just the stuff that actually works.

For readers everywhere else: I’m documenting three corners of Chinese culture that can’t be explained in a think-tank report. The food. The tea. The baijiu. One dish, one pot, one pour at a time.
